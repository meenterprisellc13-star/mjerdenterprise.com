Strategy

Strategic Focus Areas

M.J.E.R.D Enterprise targets four verticals where fragmented ownership, proven demand, and operational improvement opportunity create ideal acquisition conditions.

Why These Verticals

Disciplined Vertical Selection

Each vertical was selected based on three criteria: fragmented ownership creating acquisition opportunity, recession-resistant demand, and M.J.E.R.D’s ability to add measurable operational value post-acquisition.

🏋

Fitness & Wellness

Active Target

The U.S. fitness industry generates over $35 billion annually with thousands of independent owner-operated gyms, studios, and wellness centers operating without institutional backing. High member retention, recurring monthly revenue, and strong community attachment make fitness businesses ideal acquisition targets.

Current Activity: Ur Gains Fitness has been identified as M.J.E.R.D’s first acquisition target, generating $61,379.77 in documented gross revenue from January through October 2025.

Automotive

Pipeline

Independent auto repair shops, used car dealerships, and specialty automotive service providers represent one of the most fragmented small business categories in America. Demand is non-discretionary — vehicles require maintenance regardless of economic conditions.

M.J.E.R.D is actively sourcing automotive acquisition targets with documented revenue and a motivated owner transition opportunity.

SaaS & Technology

Pipeline

Small, profitable SaaS businesses with established customer bases, subscription revenue, and high retention rates represent some of the most attractive acquisition targets available. Low marginal cost of growth and predictable revenue make them ideal portfolio companies.

M.J.E.R.D targets niche, vertical-specific SaaS products with 50–500 paying customers and strong net revenue retention.
