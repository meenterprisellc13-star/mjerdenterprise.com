Strategy

Acquisition Criteria

M.J.E.R.D Enterprise applies rigorous, consistent criteria to every acquisition opportunity. We are disciplined buyers — not opportunistic ones.

What We Look For

Target Company Profile

We focus on owner-operated small businesses with proven cash flow, motivated sellers, and clear operational improvement opportunities.

  • Established operating history of 3+ years
  • Documented gross revenue of $200K–$5M annually
  • Positive or recoverable EBITDA with clear path to profitability
  • Owner-operator seeking exit, retirement, or transition
  • Operates within our four core verticals
  • Located in the United States with preference for Florida and Southeast
  • Strong customer retention and recurring revenue components
  • Clear operational improvement opportunities post-acquisition

Deal Structure

How We Structure Acquisitions

We employ creative capital structures that protect M.J.E.R.D’s capital while aligning incentives with sellers.

📝

Seller Financing

Preferred structure. Seller carries a portion of the purchase price, aligning their incentive with successful transition and reducing upfront capital requirement.

📈

Earnout Provisions

Performance-based contingent payments tied to post-close revenue or EBITDA targets. Protects M.J.E.R.D against valuation risk while rewarding sellers for strong transitions.

🏭

Strategic Debt

Where appropriate, M.J.E.R.D will layer SBA 7(a) loans or conventional debt alongside equity and seller financing to optimize the capital stack.

What Qualifies

Ideal Acquisition Characteristics

  • Owner motivated to sell within 12–24 months
  • Business not dependent on seller relationships to retain revenue
  • Established brand with local market presence
  • Clear, transferable operating procedures
  • Diversified customer base
  • Physical assets or intellectual property that add balance sheet value
  • Industry with recurring or repeat revenue characteristics
What Disqualifies

We Will Not Pursue

  • ✕Businesses with unresolved litigation or undisclosed liabilities
  • ✕Revenue 100% dependent on the departing owner
  • ✕Businesses outside our four core verticals
  • ✕Speculative, pre-revenue, or concept-stage ventures
  • ✕Businesses with negative EBITDA and no clear recovery path
  • ✕Transactions without adequate financial documentation
